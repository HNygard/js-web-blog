In the two years since their debut, Little Texas have emerged as one of the biggest groups in country music. Their first album went gold. Their second album went platinum. They've scored a series of hit singles including What Might Have Been, God Blessed Texas and the crossover smash My Love. And they recently won the 1994 ACM award for Top Vocal Group.

Little Texas's third album is destined to keep the group's winning streak intact. It showcases the band's increasing artistic maturity and finds their exceptional songwriting and vocal talents at their peak.

The album was produced by the band along with Christy DiNapoli and Doug Grau, who also co-produced last year's Big Time.

Little Texas will be touring steadily over the next several months. They'll take to the road in mid- October with Tim McGraw and Blackhawk. In February, they'll tour with Reba McEntire.

The band release their first home video this month. It features clips for every one of their singles (including Kick A Little) as well as exclusive new footage.
