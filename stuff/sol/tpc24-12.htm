Neil Young's first studio album since the platinum Harvest Moon finds him back with Crazy Horse for an eclectic set of songs that range from deeply personal meditations on love to clear-eyed observations on life in the 90's.

Framed by two fragile solo performances--"My Heart" and "A Dream That Can Last"--Sleeps With Angels is among the most wide-ranging albums Neil has ever recorded. Including everything from crashing rockers like "Piece Of Crap" (a bitterly funny swipe at disposable consumer culture) to the 21st-century grunge of "Blue Eden" to the classic Crazy Horse jamming on the epic "Change Your Mind," the album both acknowledges past glories and blazes new trails.

Neil and Crazy Horse were filmed extensively during the recording of the album. The footage should be reaching the public eye in various ways over the next few months.

Neil and The Horse will be touring at full gallop in the coming months. [1994, by the way, marks the 25th anniversary of their first collaboration. Tempus fugit.]
